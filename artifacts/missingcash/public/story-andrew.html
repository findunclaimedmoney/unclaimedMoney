"I assumed those old shares were worthless. After searching properly, the money was mine."

Andrew worked in the manufacturing sector for most of his career. Back in the early nineties, his employer offered a staff share scheme. He participated for a few years, then when the company restructured, he assumed the shares had either been cancelled or had gone to nothing. He never followed up.

Thirty years later, he finally did.

A Different Kind of Lost Money

Most Australians think of lost money as forgotten super. But Andrew's case is a reminder that shares, dividends, and bank account balances can also go unclaimed — transferred to state revenue offices or held by ASIC when companies can't locate their shareholders.

Did you know?

ASIC's Unclaimed Money register holds hundreds of millions in lost shares and dividends. If you've ever held shares — through an employer scheme, a float, or a broker — it's worth checking.

MissingCash's checklist directed Andrew to the ASIC Moneysmart unclaimed money search. He entered his details and found a holding registered to his old address — shares from the employee scheme that had never been transferred or cancelled, just sitting unclaimed.

Reclaiming the Shares

Andrew submitted a claim through the official ASIC process, providing identity documents and proof of his previous address. The claim was processed within a few weeks. The funds were paid directly to his bank account.

"I honestly thought I was wasting my time," he said. "I'd written those shares off decades ago. The fact that the money was still there — and that there was a proper system to claim it — was a genuine surprise."

Don't Assume It's Gone

Andrew's experience illustrates an important point: in Australia, companies are legally required to transfer unclaimed money to the government rather than keep it. That means if you've ever lost track of an investment, the money is far more likely to be sitting in a government register waiting for you than to have simply disappeared.
