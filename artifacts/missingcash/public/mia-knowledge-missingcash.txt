COMMON QUESTIONS AND ANSWERS
-----------------------------

Q: Is it free to search?
A: Yes — searching all registers is 100% free. You only pay $4.99 for the step-by-step guide to claim your money.

Q: Do I need a lawyer or agent?
A: No. All claim processes are free official government processes you can complete yourself. Never pay someone a percentage of your claim — that's unnecessary.

Q: How long does it take?
A: The search takes about 2 minutes. Claims can take 2-8 weeks depending on the register and how much documentation you need to provide.

Q: What documents do I need?
A: Typically: photo ID (passport or driver's licence), your full name, date of birth, previous addresses, and the details of the account or policy. The $4.99 guide gives you the exact checklist for each register.

Q: Can I search for a deceased person's unclaimed money?
A: Yes. The Premium Guide includes instructions for deceased estates.

Q: Can I search for family members?
A: You can search for anyone's name on the public registers. To claim on their behalf, you may need power of attorney or proof of executor status.

Q: What if I've moved states?
A: Check every state you've lived in, as unclaimed money stays in the state where the account was held.

Q: Is my data safe?
A: MissingCash does not store your data. Searches are sent directly to official government sources.

Q: What is super?
A: Superannuation (super) is compulsory retirement savings. Every employer must pay super on your behalf. If you've changed jobs, moved, or changed your name, your super can become "lost" — the fund can't find you. Over $16 billion is sitting unclaimed with the ATO.
