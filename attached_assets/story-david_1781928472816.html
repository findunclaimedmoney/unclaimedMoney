72-Year-Old Melbourne Retiree Finds Over $100,000 in Lost Super

Published by MissingCash Australia  ·  Lost Superannuation  ·  Melbourne, VIC

David T.
72 years old · Melbourne, VIC
$104,750
Total Found

When David Thompson, a 72-year-old retiree from Melbourne, decided to check his superannuation on a quiet Tuesday afternoon, he was not expecting to find anything significant. He had worked at several companies over a 40-year career and assumed any super from those jobs had long since been consolidated or lost.

What he found changed his retirement entirely.

"I thought it was a mistake at first," David said. "That money had been quietly growing while I completely forgot about those old funds. It's given us a whole new level of security in retirement."

Using the ATO's myGov portal — the same tool highlighted in the MissingCash Premium Guide — David searched for super accounts linked to his Tax File Number. The ATO showed him accounts from three previous employers, dating back to the early 1990s. With more than $104,750 sitting across forgotten accounts, David was stunned.

How He Found It

David had been aware of the concept of lost super for years but assumed the process would be complicated. After reading a step-by-step guide, he decided to give it a try. From opening myGov to viewing his full account list took less than 15 minutes.

David's Exact Steps

1
Logged in to myGovWent to my.gov.au and signed in with his existing account. Linked the ATO service (took 3 minutes).
2
Navigated to SuperClicked ATO → Super → Manage → Transfer super. Saw all funds linked to his TFN immediately.
3
Found 3 old accountsIdentified three accounts from past employers he had completely forgotten about.
4
Checked for insurance firstCalled each old fund to make sure no valuable insurance was attached before consolidating.
5
Consolidated onlineTransferred all balances to his current active fund. Done online instantly — no paperwork.

What Made the Difference

David says the key was knowing exactly what to look for and which documents to have ready. He had his Tax File Number, myGov login and Medicare card on hand. The process he followed came from a detailed checklist that walked him through every step without guesswork.

"The process was straightforward once I knew exactly what to do and which documents to prepare," he said. "I now tell all my friends and family to check — it only takes a few minutes."

David's wife also checked her accounts following the same process and found a small additional amount from a part-time job she had held in the late 1980s.

His Advice to Other Australians

"If you've worked at more than one job in your life — and who hasn't — please just check. You have no idea what might be sitting there. I genuinely couldn't believe it. My wife and I have been smiling non-stop."

David's story is not unusual. The ATO reports billions in lost and unclaimed superannuation across Australia, much of it belonging to people who have simply changed jobs, moved house or lost track of old accounts over the years.

The good news: there is no time limit on claiming. Money held by the ATO is yours indefinitely and can be reclaimed at any time with the right steps.
